Yong Zheng
(郑永)

Miller Postdoctoral Fellow
Miller Institute For Basic Research In Science, UC Berkeley, CA

Hello! I'm currently a Miller Fellow hosted by Prof. Dan Weisz in Department of Astronomy at UC Berkeley. My research interest includes galaxy formation and evolution, the CGM-galaxy interplay, and gas inflows and outflows at the disk-halo interface. I'm an observer. My research uses multi-wavelength data, including UV spectrography, optical IFU, and radio single-dish observations.

I received my Ph.D. in astronomy from Columbia University (NYC) in 2018, supervised by Prof. Mary E. Putman and Dr. Josh E. G. Peek. I received my undergraduate degree in astronomy from Peking University (Beijing) in 2012, supervised by Prof. Richard de Grijs and Prof. Xiaowei Liu.
